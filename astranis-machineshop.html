575 20th Street — Historic shipyard (Union Iron Works / Bethlehem Steel). Built warships in WWI & WWII. Now: precision aerospace manufacturing.

Building Specs

Services & Occupancy

Machine Placement · TVAC 1 & 2

One location works. One needs to move.

✅

Short LN2 piping runs · Exterior wall enables direct exhaust routing · Large open area · Adjacent to electrical room for transformer placement · Adequate rigging access.
⚠️
TVAC 2 — Center-right (near kitchen) — RELOCATE RECOMMENDED
LN2 safety: Interior location places O₂ depletion risk at most occupied area. LN2 expands 695:1 — kitchen + workbenches are highest-risk zone. Proposed Alternative — Co-locate TVAC 2 with TVAC 1
Same exterior wall → shared LN2 trunk line · shared O₂ safety zone · shared exhaust routing · single PPE station · simpler rigging path. Dramatically lower infrastructure cost.

09 / 18
Infrastructure · Liquid Nitrogen System

The largest infrastructure item on the project.

System Design
  • 2 chambers × 3 gpm = 6 gpm peak
  • Exterior bulk tank: 1,500–3,000 gallon vacuum-insulated cryogenic tank on concrete pad
  • Vacuum-insulated distribution piping to each chamber
  • Phase separator + pressure regulation at distribution point
  • Individual isolation valves + relief valves per chamber
  • Delivery truck access required — confirm pad location + turnaround
Safety Systems (mandatory)
  • Continuous O₂ monitoring — alarm at 19.5% (OSHA threshold)
  • Audible + visual alarms at each chamber + along piping routes
  • Emergency ventilation — rapid air exchange on leak event
  • PPE stations: cryo gloves, face shield, safety shoes
  • LN2 safety training for ALL personnel (not just TVAC ops)
Budget: $80K–$150K total · Long-lead: order immediately upon project approval
10 / 18
Infrastructure · Thermal / HVAC

Highest variability item — needs a leadership decision.

⚠️
Critical: Raw material MUST be stored INSIDE conditioned zone. Billets brought in from ambient will expand mid-cut, making 5µm tolerances unachievable. Makino requires 70°F ±2°F. CNC 1 at 5µm — same standard recommended.
Option A — Recommended Starting Point
Localized thermal enclosure around CNC cluster
Enclose the precision CNC zone (DMU 40 PRO units, Makino, HAAS) with insulated walls + dedicated precision HVAC. Lower cost. Protects highest-tolerance machines.
Est: $50K–$80K
Option B — More Robust
Broader zone conditioning
Condition a larger section of the shop floor. More effective for thermal equilibrium across the full material-to-machine workflow. Higher cost, more complex in a 50 ft bare-metal building.
Est: $80K–$120K
